About me

My interests center on building fast and secure systems. My research includes Parallel and Distributed Systems, Storage Systems, System Virtualization and Cloud, Operating Systems, and System Security. Currently, I am a full professor of computer science at East China Normal University (ECNU).

Before joining ECNU, I worked at Huawei Central Research Institute. As a principal researcher & technical director, I started and worked with a research group on storage systems with NVMs, and system virtualization for datacenters.

Before joining Huawei, I was an associate professor in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at Shanghai Jiao Tong University. I was also a visiting research scientist in the Department of Computer Science at Columbia University, and worked with Professor Junfeng Yang in the Software Systems Laboratory.

Research
  • Hybrid Storage System. Emerging Non-Volatile Memory (NVM) technologies can offer performance within an order of magnitude of DRAM, and make it possible to build new storage systems with NVMs and DRAM. The light-weight hybrid storing is a feasible mechanism for leveraging the emerging memory technologies, to achieve a significant performance improvement of data processing.
  • Fast Data Analysis System. Ginkgo is an in-memory distributed data management and processing system for data processing applications. We have implemented a working prototype, which can provide real-time data ingestion and data analytics on relational dataset, with a cluster of commodity servers or a cloud platform.
  • System Virtualization. System performance and system security are usually studied independently, and enforced security methods unavoidably introduce additional overhead, resulting in performance degradation. We study system security and efficiency at the same time, and attempt to establish effective-security methods to improve the cloud security while keeping its performance.
